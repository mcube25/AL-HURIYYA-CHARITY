AL-HURIYYA PROJECT

What is it

Al-Huriyya project has been setup to assist and cater for women and children in need anywhere in Nigeria. We believe every individual has a right to good health, good food, good education and true happiness. We shall bi ithnilaah empower, sponsor orphans, pay medical bills, provide meals e.t.c. Our hope and trust in Allah is that we can put smiles on faces that have been sad.

Do you want to be a part of this?

Put a smile on someones face and Allah will reward you now and in the hereafter. All you need do is to give a token donation of 250 naira a week, 1000 naira a month, 3000 naira quarterly, 6000 naira semi annually and 12000 naira annually. Its your choice ! You can also make a donation on behalf of a loved one who has passsed away.

Lets do the math

Imagine a hundred people donating 250 naira weekly,that amounts to 25000 a week, 100,000 naira monthly and 1200000 naira annually !! Inshaa Allah ! a small price to pay for a bountiful reward!

p.s: you have to be a fully registered member to take part, this is to allow for easy accountability and consistency, this is an amaanah, let us fulfil it.

registration is free and there is no membership fee. Check our contact section for registration forms and enquiries

Sisters in prison initiative

  • Prison dawah and visitation to female inmates.
  • Providing financial support to inmates who don't have the means to take care of themselves during incarceration.
  • Sourcing for and providing legal aid to inmates who don't have any form of representation or can't afford one.
  • Provision of ramadan and eid food for both male and female prisons.
  • Provision of clothes, underwear, eid clothes for the muslimat, and baby things for the prison babies.
  • Rehabilitation and assisting freed inmates to gradually integrate back into society

Support a Woman scheme

  • To work with qualified healthcare professionals to provide enlightenment on health issues related to women.
  • To work with qualified healthcare professionals to provide affordable access to qualitative obstetric and gyneacological service which will in shaa’ Allaah, reduce maternal mortality rate.
  • Provide physical and psychological support to women who are emotionally down-trodden, reverts, widowed, ill, physically challenged and living below minimum wage standard.

Durrah Projects

An educational consulting and services outfit based in Lagos. Durrah knows the importance of the foundation stage in child development and aims at working with free tuition and low income generating Nursery/Primary schools in deprived communities in Lagos state.

  • It helps with resourcing and upgrading of the schools through high quality, yet low cost teacher trainings workshops; educational material sourcing at highly competitive prices; and follow up supervision to assist the schools develop academically, morally and physically.
  • New innovations in education are researched and taught to the schools, implemented and used in giving the pupils a better methodology towards understanding the curriculum for their level.

Durrah seeks to help these school churn out fully educated, morally upright, and independent children who can compete with their counterparts from well to-do-communities and even internationally, bi ithniLlaah .Website: www.edustuffinc.wordpress.com

Daughters, wives and Mothers

It is a tough world and everyone can do with help and useful assistance. It’s with this in mind that three sisters came together as far back as 2005 to start a project that will target the young teenage girl as she grows up, develops to a young adult and eventually a full grown woman.

  • DWM was started off with our focus on the Muslim woman. However, it was soon realized that Muslim teenagers (fondly referred to as MTEENs) cannot be separated in their need for nurturing and mentoring. Thus DWM – at least the Daughters arm - decided to focus on the MTEENs first.
  • DWM is currently based in Lagos, Nigeria and will partner with other reputable organizations – Nigerian or beyond - to provide a strong base to mentor, nurture and guide Muslim teens through the murky waters of early adolescence and hope , to steer them onto the straight path in service to , themselves, the ummah and Islam.
  • DWM will grow, , from the initial step of guiding and mentoring young adults, to supporting young wives and ultimately learning with mothers till we provide total package that presents to all, the great gift of Islam. We hope to help in regaining and retaining the pure teachings of Islam, , for ourselves, our families and our ummah. Website: www.dwmdirect.com

Our modus operandi

  • We have a registration database where members are listed after filling out a form.
  • Members have the option of giving a continuous contribution of N250/week, N1000/month, N3000/quarter, N6000 twice a year, or N12000/year.
  • Monthly reminders are sent out via email and SMS whenever payment is due, 10 days to the last day of the month.
  • Members have the option of contributing on behalf of themselves or/and a loved one who has passed onThere will be reports sent to all members at the beginning and end of any project embarked on.
  • All money from the contribution is 100% used ONLY for the projects. Administrative costs (emails, SMSs, letters, adverts, etc) are not included.
  • Member can contribute towards administrative purposes seperately.

Articles

Be Unique

There is comfort in conformity, lying low and blending with the crowd. As a result, many a times, we let our world such as friends, family, television, workplaces, and all these other things define us. We keep borrowing appearances and catch whatever appears to be ‘the thing' of the season and run with it. This pretense does not get us anywhere. In fact it adds to stress as we become preoccupied with keeping up appearances and are saddled with the prospect of never living up to our potentials.

Each and every person has a being created differently to fulfill a different role and purpose: Did you think that we had created you in play (without any purpose)… (Q23:115). What is your purpose? How do you find your uniqueness?

  • Understand who you are

We tend to underestimate the importance of knowing ourselves. Many of us go through each day reacting to events and just getting by rather than making conscious choices based on who we are and what we want. We are too busy with everything else that in the end, suffocates the person inside. Self knowledge is the pillar of all values; without it, nothing else is genuine.

Is he who walks on his face (without seeing) more rightly guided, or he who (sees and) walks upright on the straight path? (Q67: 22)

To know yourself

  • Be aware of your strengths, weaknesses, values, likes and dislikes
  • Observe and be aware of your moods, reactions and responses to what is happening around you
  • Become aware of how these moods and emotions affect your state of mind
  • Examine how you interact with others
  • Observe how your environment affects you

    • Knowing and understanding yourself leads to better decision making, setting and reaching appropriate goals and altogether living more productively.

    • Trust your intuition and instincts.

    Part of knowing who you are and expressing your uniqueness is trusting your intuition and instincts. We all have an inner gauge that guides us along our most fulfilling path. Look deep within to find the answers to your life. No one is better at knowing what you need and want from life than you. Of course, it is prudent to listen to the advice of family, friends and professionals, but you are the ultimate authority on you. You are unique and original and no one but you can make your decisions for you.

    • Express yourself by cultivating your own style, tastes and personality.

    There is absolutely no need to follow the crowd. Make your life an expression of who you are. Have the courage to follow your heart. This makes you more genuine, authentic, interesting and attractive to others. Let go of fear and embrace your uniqueness. If you desire to be an Event Manager, don't settle for being an accountant because someone wants you to be, or because you can make more money at it. In the end you will lose out because the money won't matter if you're unhappy, unmotivated or depressed.

    • Believe in yourself and don't worry about what others think.

    When you choose the right path for yourself, do not allow the opinions of others to distract you. People are good at offering well-intentioned, unsolicited opinions. However only you know what's best for you. Don't let a lack of self-confidence or self-doubt prevent you from pursuing what you know is best for you. Let go of your personal insecurities. If you strive to be someone you're not, you will never be happy. Be yourself and be proud of who you are. People who have a more positive view of themselves live better, healthier lives.

    Take time with you and define yourself. Don’t wait for the world to define you. Dare to be unique, dare to be courageous, dare to be strong and vibrant, dare to be who you are, and be it the best way you know how and in shaa Allaah, you will be on the path to realize the full potential of your unique skills and talents and enjoying the fullness of life.
